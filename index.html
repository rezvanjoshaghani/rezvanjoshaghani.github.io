Hero / Intro

I am a PhD candidate in Computer Science at Boise State University. My research focuses on computational imaging and immersive visualization, with an emphasis on neural rendering, light field displays, and practical AR/VR systems for research and communication.

Research Interests

  • Neural rendering pipelines, including NeRF and Gaussian Splatting methods.
  • Light field acquisition, display, and interactive view synthesis.
  • Immersive human-computer interaction for AR/VR applications.
  • Bridging real-time graphics and machine learning for scientific visualization.

Featured Projects

NeRF and Gaussian Splatting Research

Experimental work comparing radiance-field and splatting-based scene representations with a focus on reconstruction quality, rendering speed, and reproducibility.

Luminary Visual Cave

An immersive visualization environment for presenting and evaluating 3D content in collaborative research workflows.

Light Field Viewer

An OpenGL-based viewer for exploring light field data with multi-view texture atlases and interactive camera control.

Parallax Barrier Generator

A tool for generating barrier patterns for autostereoscopic displays, enabling directional multi-view imagery from layered images.
